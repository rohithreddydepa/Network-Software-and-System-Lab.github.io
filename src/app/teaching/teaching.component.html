Goals

The immediate goal is to introduce both mature and emerging knowledge in cybersecurity, software engineering, and computer information systems to CIS/Cybersecurity students at UH, training them to become workforce-ready information systems specialists. The long-term goal includes continuous improvement of the Cybersecurity Master program and development of a Ph.D. program in the College of Technology.

Teaching Overview

Since Fall 2015, Dr. Zhang has taught two classes per semester, covering four undergraduate and two graduate courses. These courses have been consistently revised to reflect the latest in research and industry, with CIS 3355 and CIS 6397 being notably re-developed and newly developed, respectively.

Graduate Seminar

Dr. Zhang led a bi-weekly seminar with Dr. Xuqing Wu and Dr. Lian-Chieh Cheng, focusing on research presentation and discussion, fostering a collaborative environment for generating new research ideas and enhancing presentation skills among graduate students.

Advising and Recruitment

Significant contributions include advising Ph.D. and master's students and recruiting undergraduates for research projects, playing a pivotal role in their academic and professional development.

Educational Research Projects

Engagement in eight educational research projects, including a notable NSF-funded initiative, highlights Dr. Zhang's active contribution to advancing the field of cybersecurity education.
